Octopus - Octopus vulgaris

The octopus has no skeletal structure which allows it to squeeze into the tightest of openings and reach prey in the most difficult of locations. The octopus’ long tentacles, covered with hundreds of suckers, can grasp, bend and stretch easily.

Source: Octopus 101 | Suckers! by Candace Reid-Rose at the Monterey Bay Aquarium
