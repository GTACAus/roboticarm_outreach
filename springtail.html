Springtail - Hexapod

Springtails are tiny hexapods that live in soil, leaf litter, and under stones. They are not insects, but closely related. Despite their small size, they play a major role in breaking down decaying plant matter and recycling nutrients back into the ecosystem.

Springtails get their name from a forked appendage on their abdomen called a furcula. When threatened, they release this spring-loaded structure to launch themselves into the air— sometimes many times their own body length—to escape predators.


Their bodies are covered with fine scales and sensory hairs that detect moisture, vibration, and chemical cues. Springtails breathe through their skin and require damp environments to survive, which is why they are often found in gardens and compost piles.
